About HomeMade Cartoons

Comics, characters, and the artist behind it all

The Comics

Then This Happened

A slice-of-life comic series capturing those ridiculous everyday moments that make you go "did that really just happen?" From coffee disasters to grocery store encounters, these four-panel strips chronicle the chaos of modern life.

Each episode is a standalone story, perfect for quick laughs and moments of "oh yeah, I've been there too."

Bobert and the Monster

An ongoing series following Bobert, an ordinary guy who discovers his new roommate is... well, a monster. But not the scary kind. More like the "has 17 locks on their door and questionable life choices" kind.

Follow their adventures as they navigate roommate dynamics, city life, and the occasional supernatural weirdness that comes with living in Cosmopolis.

The Characters

Bobert

An everyday guy trying to live a normal life in a not-so-normal city. He's optimistic, a little naive, and surprisingly chill about having a monster for a roommate. Works a day job, dreams of something more, and usually ends up in situations he didn't sign up for.

Monster

Bobert's mysterious roommate who happens to be a monster. Secretive, paranoid (hence the 17 locks), but ultimately good-hearted. Has a complicated past they'd rather not talk about and an even more complicated present. Likes pizza, hates surprises.

You (Sort Of)

The protagonist of "Then This Happened" is basically all of us – trying to get through the day without spilling coffee on our laptops, dealing with everyday absurdities, and wondering why life has to be this way. You know, relatable chaos.

About the Artist

Hey! I'm the cartoonist behind HomeMade Cartoons. I create comics and illustrations inspired by everyday life, Mission Hill aesthetics, and the beautiful chaos of city living.

My work blends hand-drawn comic art with digital tools, aiming for that indie zine feel – a little wonky, a lot colorful, and always authentic. I'm influenced by classic comic strips, indie comics, and the neon-noir vibe of shows like Mission Hill.

When I'm not drawing comics, I'm probably spilling coffee (inspiration for "Then This Happened"), watching animated shows, or wandering around the city looking for the next story to tell.

This site is my creative playground – a place to share comics, drawings, videos of my process, and random thoughts about art and life. Thanks for stopping by Cosmopolis!
